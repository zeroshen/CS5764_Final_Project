The play store applications data has enormous potential to drive application-making businesses and companies to success. The goal of this analysis is to provide insights to android application developers about the trends in the android market and help them in creating more influential or successful applications based on various factors like rating, reviews, number of installs, etc. Actionable insights can be drawn for developers to work on and capture the android market!

Above visualization gives an analysis of the free and paid applications on the playstore. This is done by measuring the number of successful and unsuccessful applications in each category (free/ paid). For measuring the success of an application, a threshold of 1,000,000 downloads has been selected. The first insight that this visualization provides is that there is a higher chance of success for free applications than there is for paid applications. Hovering over each color in the pie chart provides further information about the number of applications. A tooltip has been employed for the same.

A new visualization was incorporated to show what price range the most successful paid applications costed. Success was measured by the number of installs, which in our case was above 1,000,000. One application costed under 3.49$ while two others costed between 3.49$ and 6.99$. It was much more probable for an application to be successful if it was free of cost as opposed to paid.

An interactive visualization has also been implemented in which a pie chart has been connected with the area chart. The purpose of this visualization is to show the growth of applications in specific categories and having specific content ratings with time. The pie charts also show what portion of the market has been captured by specific application categories and content ratings. The area chart shows us that there is a huge spike in the number of applications towards the end of 2018. The ‘family’ genre and ‘everyone’ content rating has had the most share of the market at any given point telling us that this is the sweet spot developers should focus on.

The above visualization shows the distribution of Rating values per category. This is a box plot visualization showing minimum, maximum, Quartiles, Median of Rating values for each category rating.

The innovative visualization above shows the sentiment analysis of the reviews for the applications. The general design is that each bubble represents one review and the color represents whether the sentiment is positive, negative or neutral. When we hover over the bubble, more details about the review appear. Our audience can also drag and play with the bubbles.

We help android application developers in building successful applications. We make them aware of the factors behind successful applications, and they can then incorporate them into their applications. We also explore why certain applications fail, the signs of which can be used by developers as indicators that they are heading in the wrong direction. A variety of genres are analyzed and the reason behind their success or failure is explored. Sentiments from reviews are also used in our visualization to give developers a better idea of what customers want and what to focus on while building the applications.
