  • The innovative visualization below shows the sentiment analysis of the reviews for the applications.
  • The general design is that each bubble represents one review, the color represents whether the sentiment is positive, negative or neutral and the size of the bubble represents the polarity of the review.
  • When we hover over the bubble, more details about the review appear.
  • You can also drag and play with the bubbles.

Applications that are downloaded a lot like 'Facebook' also have reviews that are a mixture of all sentiments making it harder to judge them. On the other hand, applications that are downloaded less like 'Background Wallpapers HD' have fewer and clearer sentiments in reviews. The many negative reviews for 'Facebook' can also be attributed to the fact that many users have become increasingly unhappy with the addition of ads, fake news, etc to the application with time. This is what still keeps applications like 'Amazon' and 'Google' fairly positive in their reviews, ie. customers are still happy with the service provided even though the number of downloads for these applications are the same as 'Facebook'.
