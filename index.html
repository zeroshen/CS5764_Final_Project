Analysis of Google Play Store Applications

"It's good to learn from your mistakes. It's better to learn from other people's mistakes."

- Warren Buffett

  • The play store applications data has enormous potential to drive application-making businesses and companies to success.
  • The goal of this analysis is to provide insights to android application developers about the trends in the android market and help them in creating more influential or successful applications based on various factors like rating, reviews, number of installs, etc.
  • Here, we made an assumption: If an application was downloaded for more than 10,000,000, it was a successful application.

Actionable insights can be drawn for developers to work on and capture the android market!

To measure success of an application, a threshold of 10,000,000 downloads has been selected!


Analysis of the free and paid applications on the playstore:

  • 20.7% of free applications achieved over 10,000,000 downloads.
  • Only 0.375% of paid applications achieved over 10,000,000 downloads.


There is a higher chance of success for free applications than there is for paid applications.


Growth of applications in specific categories and content ratings

  • A huge spike in the number of applications towards the end of 2018.
  • The ‘family’ genre and ‘everyone’ content rating have the most share of the market at any given point: the sweet spot developers could focus on!

  • The 'Adults only' category has the highest median rating among all categories.
  • 'Everyone' category has higher inter-quartile range.
  • Potential reason: more people are using applications that are rated 'Everyone'.

    Let's dive into more details about the customer reviewers!

    • Applications that are downloaded a lot (e.g., 'Facebook') have reviews that are a mixture of all sentiments, making it harder to judge them.
    • Applications that are downloaded less (e.g., 'Background Wallpapers HD') have fewer but clearer sentiments in reviews.
    • The many negative reviews for 'Facebook' can also be attributed to the fact that many users have become increasingly unhappy with the addition of ads, fake news, etc.
    • This is what still keeps applications like 'Amazon' and 'Google' fairly positive in their reviews, i.e., customers are still happy with the service provided even though the number of downloads for these applications are the same as 'Facebook'.


    Conclusion


    Our project aims to help android application developers in building successful applications. By becoming aware of the factors behind successful applications, they can incorporate them into their applications. In the exploration of why certain applications fail, the telltale signs can be used by developers as indicators that they are heading in the wrong direction. A variety of genres and the reasons behind their success or failure are explored. Sentiments from reviews are also visualized to give the developers a better idea of what customers want and what to focus on when developing applications.


    Takeaways:

    • Top installed application: Google, Youtube, Facebook, Instagram, Subway surfers, etc.
    • Higher chance of success in free application market.
    • 'Family' and 'Everyone' apps could be the sweet spot developers may focus on.
    • Common features that raise user dissatisfaction (directions developers could improve in the future):
      'money', 'ads', 'time', 'slow', 'useless', 'fake', etc.
